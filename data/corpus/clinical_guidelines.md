# Clinical Documentation & Guideline Reference

This document outlines general clinical documentation standards, formatting guidelines (SOAP), and safe practice guidelines for clinical scribes and documentation systems in primary care settings.

---

## SOAP Format Structure Guidelines

Clinical notes must follow the SOAP structure to ensure standardized communication across the healthcare team:

- **Subjective (S)**: Documents details of the patient's experience, chief complaint (CC), history of present illness (HPI), symptoms (onset, duration, severity, character, aggravating/relieving factors), current medications, allergies, and relevant family/social history. Sourced entirely from the patient's reports.
- **Objective (O)**: Documents measurable, observable, and physical findings. This includes vital signs (BP, heart rate, temperature, respiratory rate, oxygen saturation), physical exam observations (HEENT, lungs, cardiovascular, abdominal, musculoskeletal, neurological examinations), and point-of-care laboratory or diagnostic test results. Information must be recorded exactly as observed/documented, without inference.
- **Assessment (A)**: Contains the clinician's diagnostic reasoning, differential diagnoses, and clinical impressions. Scribes must list possibilities and options as *suggestions* for the physician's review rather than asserting a final diagnosis.
- **Plan (P)**: Outlines the management plan including medications, dosages, referrals, diagnostics (lab tests, imaging), patient education, and follow-up instructions.

## Guidelines for Defensive and Safe Documentation

### 1. No Definitive Diagnosis Assertion by Scribes
- Scribes and AI documentation systems assist the provider but do not possess clinical credentials.
- All assessments must be framed as differentials or possibilities and clearly marked with labels such as:
  - "For physician review: Possible [Condition Name] (ICD-10 suggestion: [Code] — for physician confirmation only)"
  - "Differential considerations include..."
- Never use definitive statements like "The patient has [Condition]" or "Diagnosis is [Condition]" unless the physician explicitly states a confirmed final diagnosis in the transcript.

### 2. No Extrapolating Medication Dosages
- Scribes must document *only* the specific medications, dosages, and frequencies mentioned by the physician or patient.
- Never suggest new dosages, adjust existing dosages, or make calculations (e.g., converting mg to mL or proposing pediatric weight-based adjustments) unless explicitly dictated.
- If a medication is mentioned without a dose, note the drug name and leave the dosage field blank or state "dosage not specified in transcript."

### 3. Immediate Red-Flag Identification & Escalation
- Clinical safety demands that certain high-risk symptom presentations are immediately flagged for urgent medical evaluation. Scribes must detect:
  - **Cardiac Red Flags**: Crushing chest pain, chest pain radiating to the left arm, jaw, or shoulder, chest pain associated with shortness of breath (dyspnea) or diaphoresis (sweating).
  - **Neurological Red Flags**: Sudden onset of severe headache ("thunderclap" or "worst headache of life"), focal neurological deficits (facial droop, unilateral arm/leg weakness, slurred speech or word-finding difficulties).
  - **Allergic/Airway Red Flags**: Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis) presenting with throat tightness, difficulty breathing, stridor, lip or tongue swelling.
  - **Psychiatric Red Flags**: Active suicidal ideation, expressions of self-harm intent, or homicidal thoughts.
- In these cases, systems should display a prominent warning to escalate to in-person emergency care immediately.

## ICD-10 Coding Guidelines

- **Specific vs. Unspecified Codes**: Always code to the highest level of specificity documented. For example, if a patient is diagnosed with episodic tension headaches, use G44.219 (Episodic tension-type headache, not intractable) instead of the generic G44.209 (Tension-type headache, unspecified) or R51.9 (Headache, unspecified).
- **Category Headers**: Non-billable category codes (e.g., G44.2) must not be submitted as the final billing code. They act as references.
- **Documentation Matching**: The ICD-10 code suggested must be directly supported by the clinical evidence documented in the Subjective and Objective sections. Do not speculate on or fabricate codes for unmentioned conditions.
